Solutions: Detailed Explanations

1. Solution:

Concept (Commutative & Associative Property of Multiplication): We can rearrange and group numbers in a multiplication problem to make calculations easier. Pairing numbers that result in a multiple of 10 or 100 is a common strategy.

Step 1: The original problem is $25 \times 15 \times 8 \times 4$.

Step 2: Rearrange the numbers to group compatible pairs. Let's group 25 with 4, and 15 with 8. The expression becomes $(25 \times 4) \times (15 \times 8)$.

Step 3: Calculate the value of each group. $25 \times 4 = 100$ and $15 \times 8 = 120$.

Step 4: Multiply the results: $100 \times 120 = 12000$.

Final Answer: 12000

2. Solution:

Concept (Commutative & Associative Property of Addition): We can reorder and group numbers in an addition problem to simplify the calculation, often by pairing numbers that add up to a round number.

Step 1: The problem is $199 + 345 + 101 + 655$.

Step 2: Reorder the numbers to group pairs that are easy to add. Let's group 199 with 101, and 345 with 655. The expression is now $(199 + 101) + (345 + 655)$.

Step 3: Calculate the sum of each group. $199 + 101 = 300$ and $345 + 655 = 1000$.

Step 4: Add the results: $300 + 1000 = 1300$.

Final Answer: 1300

3. Solution:

Concept (Associative Property): We can break down numbers into factors to simplify multiplication. A useful trick is to use the fact that $125 = 1000 \div 8$.

Step 1: The problem is $125 \times 56$.

Step 2: Substitute $125$ with $(1000 \div 8)$. The expression becomes $(1000 \div 8) \times 56$.

Step 3: Rearrange the operations: $1000 \times (56 \div 8)$.

Step 4: Perform the division first: $56 \div 8 = 7$.

Step 5: Perform the final multiplication: $1000 \times 7 = 7000$.

Final Answer: 7000

4. Solution:

Concept (Distributive Property): We can express a number as a sum or difference and distribute the multiplication over it. For example, $a \times (b + c) = a \times b + a \times c$.

Step 1: The problem is $45 \times 102$.

Step 2: Rewrite 102 as $(100 + 2)$. The expression becomes $45 \times (100 + 2)$.

Step 3: Apply the distributive property: $(45 \times 100) + (45 \times 2)$.

Step 4: Calculate each part: $45 \times 100 = 4500$ and $45 \times 2 = 90$.

Step 5: Add the results: $4500 + 90 = 4590$.

Final Answer: 4590

5. Solution:

Concept (Distributive Property in Reverse): We can factor out a common term from a sum of products. $a \times b + a \times c = a \times (b + c)$.

Step 1: The problem is $137 \times 43 + 137 \times 57$.

Step 2: Notice that 137 is a common factor. We can use the distributive property to factor it out: $137 \times (43 + 57)$.

Step 3: Calculate the sum inside the parentheses: $43 + 57 = 100$.

Step 4: Perform the final multiplication: $137 \times 100 = 13700$.

Final Answer: 13700

6. Solution:

Concept (Order of Operations and Factoring): Simplify calculations by factoring numbers and cancelling common factors before performing large multiplications.

Step 1: The problem is $250 \times 64 \div 160$.

Step 2: Rewrite the division as a fraction: $\frac{250 \times 64}{160}$.

Step 3: We can simplify by dividing 64 and 160 by their greatest common divisor, 32. $64 \div 32 = 2$ and $160 \div 32 = 5$. The expression becomes $\frac{250 \times 2}{5}$.

Step 4: Now calculate the result: $\frac{500}{5} = 100$.

Final Answer: 100

19. Solution:

Concept (Applying Multiple Properties): Break down a complex problem into simpler parts using properties of numbers like $25 = 100 \div 4$ and factoring.

Step 1: The problem is $375 \times 104 + 640 \times 25 - 625 \times 56$.

Step 2 (Part 1): Calculate $375 \times 104 = 375 \times (100+4) = 37500 + 1500 = 39000$.

Step 3 (Part 2): Calculate $640 \times 25 = 640 \times (100 \div 4) = (640 \div 4) \times 100 = 160 \times 100 = 16000$.

Step 4 (Part 3): Calculate $625 \times 56$. Note that $625 \times 8 = 5000$. So, $625 \times (8 \times 7) = (625 \times 8) \times 7 = 5000 \times 7 = 35000$.

Step 5 (Combine): The expression is now $39000 + 16000 - 35000$.

Step 6: Calculate the final result: $55000 - 35000 = 20000$.

Final Answer: 20000

20. Solution:

Concept (Arithmetic Series & Distributive Property): The sum is an arithmetic series with a common difference. We can factor out the common difference and sum the remaining sequence.

Step 1: The problem is $7+14+21+...+63+70+77$.

Step 2: Notice that every number is a multiple of 7. Factor out 7: $7 \times (1+2+3+...+9+10+11)$.

Step 3: We need to find the sum of the numbers from 1 to 11. The formula for the sum of the first 'n' integers is $\frac{n(n+1)}{2}$. Here, n=11.

Step 4: Calculate the sum: $\frac{11 \times (11+1)}{2} = \frac{11 \times 12}{2} = 11 \times 6 = 66$.

Step 5: Multiply this sum by the factor we took out: $7 \times 66$.

Step 6: $7 \times 66 = 7 \times (60 + 6) = 420 + 42 = 462$.

Final Answer: 462
