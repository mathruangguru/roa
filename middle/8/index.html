Solution for Question 1

Concept: This is an 'Excess and Shortage' problem. We find the total difference between the two scenarios and divide it by the difference in items given per group to find the number of groups (students).

Solution Steps:

  1. Difference per Student: In the first case, each student gets 4 sweets. In the second, 6 sweets. The difference in sweets per student is $6 - 4 = 2$ sweets.
  2. Total Difference: In the first case, there's an excess of 8 sweets (+8). In the second, there's a shortage of 12 sweets (-12). The total swing or difference between these two outcomes is $8 + 12 = 20$ sweets. This total difference is caused by giving each student 2 extra sweets.
  3. Find Number of Students: To find the number of students, we divide the total difference in sweets by the difference per student. Number of students = $20 \div 2 = 10$.
Final Answer: There are 10 students in the class.

Solution for Question 2

Concept: If giving away some items makes the amounts equal, the initial difference is twice the amount given away. We can find the individual amounts if we know their sum and difference.

Solution Steps:

  1. Find the Difference: Andy gives 10 marbles to Ben to make them equal. This means Andy initially had more. The 10 marbles taken from Andy and given to Ben covered the gap. So, the initial difference was $10 \times 2 = 20$ marbles. Andy had 20 more marbles than Ben.
  2. Find the 'Equal' Amount: If they had an equal number of marbles, each would have $80 \div 2 = 40$ marbles.
  3. Find Andy's Initial Amount: The equal amount (40) is the midpoint. The difference is 20, so half the difference is $20 \div 2 = 10$. Andy's initial amount is the midpoint plus half the difference: $40 + 10 = 50$. (Ben's would be $40 - 10 = 30$).
  4. Verification: Initially, Andy has 50, Ben has 30. Total is 80. If Andy gives 10 to Ben, Andy has $50 - 10 = 40$ and Ben has $30 + 10 = 40$. They are equal.
Final Answer: Andy had 50 marbles at first.

Solution for Question 3

Concept: This is another 'Excess and Shortage' problem. We first find the number of rows, then use that information to calculate the total number of chairs.

Solution Steps:

  1. Difference per Row: In the first case, 10 chairs per row. In the second, 9 chairs per row. The difference is $10 - 9 = 1$ chair per row.
  2. Total Difference: In the first case, there's a shortage of 3 chairs (-3). In the second, there's an excess of 5 chairs (+5). The total difference is $3 + 5 = 8$ chairs.
  3. Find Number of Rows: Number of rows = Total Difference $\div$ Difference per Row = $8 \div 1 = 8$ rows.
  4. Calculate Total Chairs: We can use either scenario.
    Using scenario 1: (8 rows $\times$ 10 chairs/row) - 3 (shortage) = $80 - 3 = 77$ chairs.
    Using scenario 2: (8 rows $\times$ 9 chairs/row) + 5 (excess) = $72 + 5 = 77$ chairs.
Final Answer: There are 77 chairs in total.

Solution for Question 5

Concept: The total number of apples remains constant even after the transfer. We can use the final ratio to find the amounts after the transfer, and then work backward to find the initial amounts.

Solution Steps:

  1. Total is Constant: The total number of apples is always 240.
  2. After Transfer: After the transfer, Basket B has 3 times as many as Basket A. This is a ratio of B:A = 3:1. The total number of units is $3 + 1 = 4$ units.
  3. Value of One Unit: 4 units represent 240 apples. So, 1 unit = $240 \div 4 = 60$ apples.
  4. Apples After Transfer: After the transfer, Basket A has 1 unit = 60 apples. Basket B has 3 units = $3 \times 60 = 180$ apples.
  5. Find Initial Amount in Basket A: The 60 apples in Basket A is the amount *after* giving away 20 apples. So, initially, Basket A had $60 + 20 = 80$ apples.
Final Answer: There were 80 apples in Basket A at first.

    2. Hitung Total Tinggi Tepi: Tinggi total tumpukan adalah 24 cm. Total tinggi semua tepi adalah selisih antara tinggi tumpukan dan total ketebalan piring. Total tinggi tepi = $24 - 15 = 9$ cm.
    3. Cari Jumlah Interval (Tepi): Untuk tumpukan 10 piring, ada $10 - 1 = 9$ tepi (atau interval) yang menciptakan ruang di antara mereka.
    4. Cari Tinggi Satu Tepi: Total tinggi 9 tepi adalah 9 cm. Tinggi satu tepi = Total tinggi tepi $\div$ Jumlah tepi = $9 \div 9 = 1$ cm.
    Jawaban Akhir: Tinggi tepi setiap piring adalah 1 cm.
