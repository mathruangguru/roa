Solutions / Pembahasan

Key Concept: Singapore Math - Bar Models

Singapore Math commonly uses Bar Models to visually represent abstract geometric or algebraic ratios, simplifying complex logic into tangible proportional units.

Konsep Kunci: Singapore Math - Model Bar

Metode Singapore Math sangat sering menggunakan Model Bar untuk memvisualisasikan rasio geometri atau aljabar yang abstrak, menyederhanakan logika rumit menjadi unit proporsi yang mudah dipahami.

EN1. Solution:

  • Step 1 (Bar Model): The exterior angle and interior angle form a straight line ($180^\circ$). Let the exterior angle be 1 unit box. Thus, the interior angle is 17 unit boxes.
  • Step 2 (Total Units): $17 \text{ units} + 1 \text{ unit} = 18 \text{ units} = 180^\circ$.
  • Step 3 (Value per Unit): $180^\circ \div 18 = 10^\circ$ (This is the exterior angle).
  • Step 4 (Number of sides $n$): The formula for the number of sides is $360^\circ$ divided by its exterior angle. $n = 360^\circ \div 10^\circ = 36$.
Final Answer: 36

ID1. Langkah Penyelesaian:

  • Langkah 1 (Model Visual): Sudut luar dan sudut dalam membentuk garis lurus ($180^\circ$). Anggap sudut luar sebagai 1 unit kotak. Maka sudut dalam adalah 17 unit kotak.
  • Langkah 2 (Total Unit): $17 \text{ unit} + 1 \text{ unit} = 18 \text{ unit} = 180^\circ$.
  • Langkah 3 (Nilai 1 Unit): $180^\circ \div 18 = 10^\circ$ (Ini adalah besar sudut luar).
  • Langkah 4 (Banyak Sisi $n$): Rumus paten banyak sisi poligon adalah $360^\circ$ dibagi sudut luarnya. $n = 360^\circ \div 10^\circ = 36$.
Jawaban Akhir: 36

  • Step 1: Find the squared lengths of the sides of $\triangle ABC$ using the distance formula. $A=(0,4), B=(6,2), C=(10,4)$.
  • Step 2: $AB^2 = (0-6)^2 + (4-2)^2 = 36 + 4 = 40$.
    $BC^2 = (10-6)^2 + (4-2)^2 = 16 + 4 = 20$.
    $AC^2 = (10-0)^2 + (4-4)^2 = 100 + 0 = 100$.
  • Step 3 (Law of Cosines): $AC^2 = AB^2 + BC^2 - 2(AB)(BC)\cos(\angle ABC)$.
    $100 = 40 + 20 - 2(\sqrt{40})(\sqrt{20})\cos(\angle ABC)$.
  • Step 4: $100 = 60 - 2(\sqrt{800})\cos(\angle ABC) \implies 40 = -40\sqrt{2}\cos(\angle ABC) \implies \cos(\angle ABC) = -\frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}$.

Final Answer: $135^\circ$
