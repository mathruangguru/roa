Solutions for Part 1: Pigeonhole Principle

Solution Number 1:

Concept (Singapore Math Logic): To master the Pigeonhole Principle, always model the Worst-Case Scenario. Imagine you are extremely unlucky and try to delay success for as long as mathematically possible. The variables are Pigeons (items taken) and Holes (categories).

Konsep: Untuk menguasai Prinsip Sangkar Merpati, selalu modelkan Skenario Terburuk. Bayangkan Anda sangat tidak beruntung dan cobalah menunda keberhasilan selama mungkin secara matematis. Variabel utamanya adalah Merpati (benda yang diambil) dan Sangkar (kategori).

Solution (English):

Identify the 'Holes': We have 2 color categories (Black and Blue). Therefore, Holes = 2.

Identify the 'Pigeons': The socks we are drawing out.

Model the Worst-Case Scenario: You want a matching pair, so the universe forces you to pick different colors continuously.

Draw 1: You get a Black sock.

Draw 2: You get a Blue sock. You now have 2 socks, but no pair is formed.

Since all holes (colors) are now occupied by exactly 1 pigeon, the 3rd draw must match one of the colors already in your hand, forming a pair.

Langkah Penyelesaian (Indonesia):

Tentukan 'Sangkar': Kita memiliki 2 kategori warna (Hitam dan Biru). Jadi, Sangkar = 2.

Tentukan 'Merpati': Kaus kaki yang akan ditarik keluar.

Modelkan Skenario Terburuk: Kita ingin sepasang warna sama, jadi alam semesta 'memaksa' kita selalu mengambil warna yang berbeda secara berurutan.

Pengambilan ke-1: Dapat 1 Hitam.

Pengambilan ke-2: Dapat 1 Biru. Sekarang kita punya 2 kaus kaki, tapi belum ada yang sepasang.

Karena semua sangkar (warna) sudah terisi tepat 1 merpati, pengambilan ke-3 pasti akan sama dengan salah satu warna di tangan kita. Pasangan terbentuk!

Answer: 3 socks. / Jawaban: 3 kaus kaki.

At this exact breaking point (107 balls), the very next ball you pull (108th) MUST be Blue, Red, or Green, immediately pushing that color's count to 30.

Analyze Target B: We need 2 pairs of Non-blue. "Non-blue" comes from White (22) and Yellow (18). To guarantee 2 valid pairs from this mix, what is the maximum we can draw while FAILING? Worst-Case Scenario 2 (Depriving Non-blue): The universe hides the Non-blue chopsticks. We draw all Blue first ($39$). Then we draw the 5 required Non-blue ones. Total = $39 + 5 = 44$.

Since both failure paths peak at 44, the 44th draw definitively satisfies both targets.

Concept: Map numerical relationships into pairs (Pigeonholes). Numbers that cannot form a valid pair must be separated as "unpaired elements".

Konsep: Petakan relasi numerik menjadi pasangan-pasangan (Sangkar). Angka yang tidak bisa membentuk pasangan valid harus dipisahkan sebagai "elemen jomblo/tidak berpasangan".

Solution (English):

Determine valid pairs summing to 244 from the range [55, 159]. The pairs start from the extremes: (159, 85), (158, 86), counting down until they meet closely at (123, 121).

Count these paired groups: From 85 to 121, there are $121 - 85 + 1 = 37$ distinct pairs. This utilizes 74 numbers.

Identify "unpaired" numbers: Numbers from 55 up to 84 (30 numbers) lack a partner because their required matching half is $>159$. Also, the number 122 requires another 122, but repetition isn't allowed. Total unpaired = $30 + 1 = 31$ numbers.

Worst-Case Strategy: Draw ALL 31 unpaired numbers (useless for sums), AND draw exactly one half from each of the 37 available valid pairs (still no sum to 244!).

$$ \begin{aligned} \text{Total holding} &= 31 (\text{unpaired}) + 37 (\text{half-pairs}) \\ &= 68 \text{ numbers} \end{aligned} $$

The 69th draw must forcibly be the missing half to one of the 37 half-pairs in your hand.

Langkah Penyelesaian (Indonesia):

Tarikan ke-69 secara otomatis akan menjadi belahan yang melengkapi salah satu dari 37 angka yang sudah ditangan.

Goal: Maximize the gap between the richest and poorest player.

Constraint: Every player gets a minimum of 6 chips, and all 35 players must have strictly distinct amounts.

Stingy Distribution Model: Force the first 34 players to take the absolute bare minimum sequence starting from 6.

Sequence: 6, 7, 8, ... up to the 34th player, whose value is $6 + (34 - 1) = 39$.

Calculate the total chips locked up by these 34 poorest players using the sum of arithmetic progression formula:

$$ \begin{aligned} \text{Sum} &= \frac{n}{2} \times (\text{First} + \text{Last}) \\ &= \frac{34}{2} \times (6 + 39) \\ &= 17 \times 45 = 765 \text{ chips} \end{aligned} $$

The 35th player takes the massive remainder: $3506 - 765 = 2741$ chips.

The maximum difference is Richest (2741) minus Poorest (6) = 2735.

$$ \begin{aligned} \text{Jumlah} &= \frac{n}{2} \times (\text{Awal} + \text{Akhir}) \\ &= \frac{34}{2} \times (6 + 39) \\ &= 17 \times 45 = 765 \text{ chip} \end{aligned} $$

Pemain ke-35 memboyong sisa raksasanya: $3506 - 765 = 2741$ chip.

Solution Number 7:

Concept: Modulo (Remainders) mathematically map perfectly to Pigeonholes. If two numbers share the same remainder when divided by $k$, their difference is purely divisible by $k$.

Konsep: Modulo (Sisa bagi) secara matematis memetakan dengan sempurna ke Konsep Sangkar. Jika dua angka memiliki sisa bagi yang sama ketika dibagi $k$, selisih keduanya murni habis dibagi $k$.

Solution (English):

Define the Holes: When any integer is divided by 3, there are only 3 possible remainders it can leave: 0, 1, or 2. These represent our 3 Holes.

Define the Pigeons: We are arbitrarily selecting 4 integers.

Applying PHP: Since we are placing 4 integers (Pigeons) into 3 remainders (Holes), the principle guarantees that at least 2 integers must share the exact same remainder.

Algebraic Proof: Suppose integer $A$ and integer $B$ both leave a remainder of $R$ when divided by 3. We can write them as $A = 3x + R$ and $B = 3y + R$.

When subtracted: $(A - B) = (3x + R) - (3y + R) = 3(x - y)$. The remainders cancel out perfectly, proving the difference is divisible by 3.

Langkah Penyelesaian (Indonesia):

Tentukan Sangkar: Jika bilangan bulat dibagi 3, kemungkinan sisa baginya hanya ada 3: 0, 1, atau 2. Ini mewakili 3 Sangkar kita.

Tentukan Merpati: Kita memilih 4 bilangan bulat secara acak.

Menerapkan PHP: Karena kita memasukkan 4 bilangan (Merpati) ke dalam 3 kemungkinan sisa (Sangkar), prinsip ini menjamin minimal 2 bilangan pasti masuk ke sangkar sisa yang sama.

Pembuktian Aljabar: Misalkan bilangan $A$ dan $B$ sama-sama memiliki sisa $R$ jika dibagi 3. Format angkanya adalah $A = 3x + R$ dan $B = 3y + R$.

Jika dikurangkan: $(A - B) = (3x + R) - (3y + R) = 3(x - y)$. Sisa $R$ saling meniadakan menjadi nol, membuktikan selisihnya habis dibagi 3.

Answer: Proven logically. / Jawaban: Terbukti logis.
