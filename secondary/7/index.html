Solution for Question 1

Concept: When two parallel lines are intersected by a transversal, the consecutive interior angles (angles on the same side of the transversal and between the parallel lines) are supplementary, meaning they add up to $180^\circ$. Also, alternate interior angles are equal.

Solution Steps:

  1. Identify the relationship: $\angle 1$ and the angle vertically opposite to $\angle 3$ are alternate interior angles. Therefore, their measures are equal. Let's call the angle vertically opposite to $\angle 3$ as $\angle 4$. So, $\angle 1 = \angle 4$. Since $\angle 3$ and $\angle 4$ are vertically opposite, $\angle 3 = \angle 4$. This means $\angle 1 = \angle 3$.
  2. Alternative relationship: Angles $\angle 1$ and $\angle 2$ are consecutive interior angles. Their sum must be $180^\circ$.
  3. Set up the equation: Using the consecutive interior angles property: $(3x + 15) + (5x - 25) = 180$.
  4. Solve for x: Combine like terms: $8x - 10 = 180$. Add 10 to both sides: $8x = 190$. Divide by 8: $x = \frac{190}{8} = 23.75$.
  5. Find the measure of $\angle 1$: $\angle 1 = 3x + 15 = 3(23.75) + 15 = 71.25 + 15 = 86.25^\circ$.
  6. Find the measure of $\angle 3$: As established in step 1, $\angle 3$ and $\angle 1$ are alternate interior angles (or can be seen as corresponding to the angle vertically opposite to $\angle 1$, etc.). Therefore, $\angle 3 = \angle 1$. So, $\angle 3 = 86.25^\circ$. We can also see that $\angle 2$ and $\angle 3$ are supplementary. $\angle 2 = 5(23.75) - 25 = 118.75-25 = 93.75^\circ$. Then $\angle 3 = 180 - 93.75 = 86.25^\circ$.
Final Answer: The measure of $\angle 3$ is $86.25^\circ$.

Solution for Question 2

Concept: The measure of an exterior angle of a triangle is equal to the sum of the measures of its two remote interior angles (the interior angles that are not adjacent to the exterior angle).

Solution Steps:

  1. Identify the angles: The exterior angle at vertex $Q$ is given as $115^\circ$. The two remote interior angles are $\angle P$ and $\angle R$.
  2. Apply the Exterior Angle Theorem: According to the theorem, Exterior Angle at $Q = \angle P + \angle R$.
  3. Set up the equation: We are given the values for the exterior angle at $Q$ and for $\angle P$. So, $115^\circ = 50^\circ + \angle R$.
  4. Solve for $\angle R$: Subtract $50^\circ$ from both sides of the equation: $\angle R = 115^\circ - 50^\circ = 65^\circ$.
Final Answer: The measure of $\angle R$ is $65^\circ$.

Solution for Question 3

Concept: In an isosceles triangle, angles opposite to the equal sides are equal. The sum of angles in any triangle is $180^\circ$.

Solution Steps:

  1. Analyze $\triangle ABD$: We are given $AD = BD$. This means $\triangle ABD$ is an isosceles triangle. Therefore, the angles opposite to these sides are equal: $\angle B = \angle BAD$. Let's call this angle $x$. So, $\angle B = \angle BAD = x$.
  2. Analyze $\triangle ABC$: We are given $AB = AC$. This means $\triangle ABC$ is an isosceles triangle. Therefore, the angles opposite to these sides are equal: $\angle B = \angle C$. Since $\angle B = x$, we have $\angle C = x$.
  3. Express angles in terms of x: We have $\angle B = x$ and $\angle C = x$. The entire angle $\angle BAC$ is the sum of $\angle BAD$ and $\angle DAC$. So, $\angle BAC = \angle BAD + \angle DAC = x + 30^\circ$.
  4. Apply the angle sum property to $\triangle ABC$: The sum of angles in $\triangle ABC$ is $180^\circ$. So, $\angle B + \angle C + \angle BAC = 180^\circ$.
  5. Set up and solve the equation: Substitute the expressions in terms of $x$: $x + x + (x + 30^\circ) = 180^\circ$.
    $3x + 30^\circ = 180^\circ$
    $3x = 150^\circ$
    $x = 50^\circ$.
  6. Conclusion: Since we defined $\angle B = x$, the measure of $\angle B$ is $50^\circ$.
Final Answer: The measure of $\angle B$ is $50^\circ$.

Solution for Question 4

Concept: The Angle Bisector Theorem states that if a ray bisects an angle of a triangle, then it divides the opposite side into two segments that are proportional to the other two sides of the triangle.

Solution Steps:

  1. State the Angle Bisector Theorem for $\triangle ABC$: Since $AD$ is the angle bisector of $\angle A$, the theorem gives the proportion: $\frac{AB}{AC} = \frac{BD}{DC}$.
  2. Define the segment lengths: We are given $AB=10$, $AC=14$, and $BC=18$. Let the length of $BD$ be $x$. Then the length of $DC$ must be the remainder of $BC$, which is $18 - x$.
  3. Set up the equation: Substitute the known values and expressions into the proportion: $\frac{10}{14} = \frac{x}{18-x}$.
  4. Solve for x: Simplify the fraction on the left: $\frac{5}{7} = \frac{x}{18-x}$. Cross-multiply: $5(18-x) = 7x$. Distribute on the left side: $90 - 5x = 7x$. Add $5x$ to both sides: $90 = 12x$. Divide by 12: $x = \frac{90}{12} = \frac{15}{2} = 7.5$.
  5. Conclusion: Since $x$ represents the length of $BD$, the length of $BD$ is 7.5.
Final Answer: The length of $BD$ is 7.5.
